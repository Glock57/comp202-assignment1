University of Waikato Notices

3MT Doctoral finals – Wednesday 16 Aug, 7pm

The Three Minute Thesis (3MT) Doctoral competition finals are on Wednesday 16 August at 7pm in the Academy and features the 10 finalists selected from the heats held in July. Entry to the finals is free, no RSVP required. Kahurangi Craft (cash bar) will be open from 6pm and the Concert Chamber doors from 6.45pm for a 7pm start. Come along and support the University’s future researchers!
More information at the 3MT website

TEDxRuakura - Sunday 20 August, GAPA

Hamilton’s third annual TEDx event, TEDxRuakura, is on Sunday 20 August at the Academy. The University is honoured to host and sponsor the event and will once again be represented among the list of speakers, which includes Professor Michael Peters (FEDU) and Associate Professor Ryan Ko (FCMS), as well as alumni Tania Jones and Linda Radosinska. Tickets to the event are extremely limited so get in quick.
Buy tickets from the TEDxRuakura website

Lunchtime Recital Series: Student Concert – Wednesday 16 August, 1pm

Top Conservatorium of Music students inspire and delight with solo and chamber instrumental works and a variety of vocal repertoire.
Location: Dr John Gallagher Concert Chamber, Gallagher Academy of Performing Arts
Entry: $5 at the door, free for students with a valid ID.
More information at the Music homepage

HSBC Sevens, Hamilton, 3-4 February 2018: Early bird tickets available now

As loyal supporters of the Chiefs, for the month of August, University staff and students can purchase tickets to the HSBC New Zealand Rugby Sevens at FMG Stadium Waikato, Hamilton, on Saturday 3 & Sunday 4 February 2018, before they go on sale to the general public. Your preferential pre-buy window is open now and runs until 11.59pm on Tuesday 29 August 2017. Tickets go on public sale from midday Wednesday 30 August 2017. Simply visit www.sevens.co.nz, choose your seat and enter the code below.
Click here and enter the promo code 7SCHIEFS on the purchase page

Tauranga Lecture Series: Europe in Crisis - Thursday 17 Aug, 6pm

The second Tauranga Lecture Series event feautres Dr Simona Soare (Security and Defence Advisor to the VP of the European Parliament) who will explore how the integration of Europe is threatened and how democracy is in danger of being rolled back under the impact of these threats. Her lecture ‘Europe in crisis: the impact of populism, migration and terrorism’ is on Thursday 17 August at 6pm at Trinity Wharf, 51 Dive Crescent, Tauranga. Doors open 5.30pm.

Conservatorium of Music German Lieder Festival and Workshops: 28-30 August

Announcing the new annual German Lieder Festival and Workshops, with visiting artist and tutor Penelope Cashman, a specialist in German Lieder diction and interpretation. Voice students will participate in workshops and coaching across the three days, culminating in a lieder recital with selected works. Taking place during the Lunchtime Recital Series concert on August 30, 1pm at the Academy. All are welcome to attend the series of workshops, please register your interest at music@waikato.ac.nz
More information at the Music homepage

Inaugural Lecture: Windows to other worlds - Tuesday 15 Aug at 5.15pm

Could the rich and diverse landscape of microorganisms living under the ice of the Antarctic lakes hold the key for understanding how life began on Earth? And what about the potential for extra-terrestrial life? Professor of Biology Ian Hawes will discuss what can be learnt from these and other ‘analogue sites’ at his Inaugural Professorial Lecture "Windows to other worlds: Antarctic lakes, astrobiology and milestone moments for life on ancient earth" on Tuesday 15 August, 5.15pm at the Academy.
More information about Inaugural Professorial Lectures
